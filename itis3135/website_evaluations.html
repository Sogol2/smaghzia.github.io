Website Evaluations

Apple

Apple’s marketing site highlights products with bold imagery, minimal copy, and consistent grid/spacing. It’s a clean example of visual hierarchy.

  • Contrast: Large hero images vs. small body text; heavy weight headlines vs. light body copy make priorities obvious.
  • Repetition: Reused type scale, button styles, grid gutters, and ample white space unify pages.
  • Alignment: Precise left/center alignment and consistent columns keep the eye moving predictably.
  • Proximity: Headlines, copy, and CTAs are tightly grouped per product; unrelated blocks have generous spacing.

Ling’s Cars

Infamously busy layout with animation, clashing colors, and dense clusters of elements. Memorable, but breaks most fundamentals.

  • Contrast: So many competing colors, sizes, and effects that nothing clearly stands out as primary.
  • Repetition: Few consistent patterns; many one-off badges, fonts, and buttons produce visual noise.
  • Alignment: Elements stack in irregular blocks with mixed alignments, weakening the reading flow.
  • Proximity: Unrelated items are crammed together; related info is often split apart, making scanning hard.
